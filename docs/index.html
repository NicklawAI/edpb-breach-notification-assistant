EDPB breach notification: a proposal, and a tool you can use today

Not just a critique - we ship a reference implementation

The paper endorses the template's strengths and proposes 21 additive improvements, drawn from a comparison with mature regimes (UK, Australia, California, Canada) and standards (ENISA, ISO/IEC 27035). The accompanying tool demonstrates that the technical proposals - a machine-readable schema, ENISA severity scoring, an open submission API - are concrete and usable now.

The 21 proposals

  1. SME track (Art. 33(3) minimum)
  2. Anchor severity to ENISA (SE ≥ 2)
  3. Align the severity scale (3 vs 4 levels)
  4. Format, not channel
  5. Structured withdrawal reason
  6. Processor-to-controller flag (Art. 33(2))
  7. Encryption → Art. 34(3)(a) link
  8. Multi-regime coordination (NIS2/DORA)
  9. Attachment format/size guidance
  10. Two-tier public/confidential (OAIC)
  11. Guided pre-assessment (ICO)
  12. Art. 33(5) register (Canada, 24 months)
  13. ISO/IEC 27035 alignment
  14. Aggregate transparency (California)
  15. Transition timeline
  16. Machine-readable format + optional M2M
  17. Digital Omnibus forward-compatibility
  18. NIS2/DORA staged-timeline mapping
  19. Machine-computable severity
  20. Multilingual stable vocabulary
  21. Multi-value field cardinality (C/I/A, categories)
