**2. What data we collect.** When you submit the adherence form you provide: your name, your professional role, your organisation, your country, and an optional free-text comment. The GitHub username used to submit the form is also inherently associated with your submission.

**3. Why we process it (purpose).** Solely to register and display your adherence/endorsement to the position paper submitted to the European Data Protection Board (EDPB) public consultation, and to demonstrate the breadth of support to the EDPB and EU institutions.

**4. Public disclosure - please read carefully.** Your adherence is PUBLIC. The data you submit is published in the public GitHub Issue you create and in the SIGNATORIES.md file of this repository, and is therefore accessible to anyone who visits the repository, including the EDPB, EU institutions and any internet user worldwide. Do not include in the optional comment any personal data, confidential or sensitive information that you do not wish to be publicly visible.

**5. Legal basis.** Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR). Adherence is entirely voluntary; you give your consent by submitting the form and ticking the consent box. You may withdraw your consent at any time (see point 7).

**6. Retention.** Your data is kept for as long as the position paper remains relevant to the EDPB consultation and its follow-up. Afterwards the signatories list may be archived or removed.

**7. Your rights.** Under Articles 15 to 22 GDPR you have the right of access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17, including removal from the signatories list), restriction (Art. 18), objection (Art. 21) and data portability (Art. 20), and the right to withdraw your consent at any time under Article 7(3) (without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal). To exercise these rights, email nicola@franchetto.legal: we will remove you from SIGNATORIES.md and close or redact the related Issue. Please note honestly: because the data was made public on GitHub, we cannot guarantee its removal from third-party copies, forks, caches or web archives outside our control.

**8. Right to complain.** You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular in the EU Member State of your residence, place of work or of the alleged infringement (in Italy, the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali, garanteprivacy.it).

**9. Hosting and international transfer.** The repository is hosted by GitHub, Inc. (a Microsoft company) on infrastructure that includes the United States. By adhering, you understand that your data is hosted and made publicly available on GitHub's platform, including outside the EU/EEA.

**10. No automated decision-making.** Your data is not subject to automated decision-making or profiling, and is not used for marketing or any purpose other than the one stated in point 3.
